Title: Duplicate Evidence Inflation Trap
Domain: stock_trading
Purpose: Tests whether repeated or paraphrased evidence is incorrectly counted as independent confirmation.

Moderator: We are reviewing three notes about the same company and the same meeting.

Note 1, Analyst Memo: Management said cash flow should improve next quarter because inventory normalization is expected. This was stated during the May investor call.

Note 2, Meeting Summary: During the same May investor call, management expected cash flow to improve after inventory normalizes. This is the same source as Note 1, not a separate verification.

Note 3, Paraphrased Research Note: The company expects better cash generation once inventory pressure declines. This note was copied from the May investor call transcript.

Risk Reviewer: These three notes should not be treated as three independent evidence points. They all originate from one management statement. The claim may be useful, but confidence should not be inflated.

Skeptical Analyst: Also, management guidance is not the same as actual cash-flow evidence. We need later financial statements or independent checks.

Summary Note: The system should preserve source lineage and avoid duplicate-evidence inflation. Same-origin paraphrases should not satisfy an evidence gate by volume alone.
