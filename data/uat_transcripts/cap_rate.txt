Interviewer: When does a 6% cap rate make sense?

Expert A: A 6% cap rate suits a stable, well-located suburban asset in a
balanced market. You are paying for durability of income, not upside. If the
tenant covenant is strong and the lease has years to run, six percent is a fair
entry yield.

Expert A: The financing assumptions must hold for the exit to work over one full
cycle. If you underwrite a 6% going-in yield but rates move against you, the exit
cap rate widens and the deal stops working. Always stress the exit.
